How to Play

The Basics

Two players take turns placing cards on a 3×3 grid. Each card has four numbers—one for each side (top, right, bottom, left)—and one or two elemental types.

When you place a card next to an opponent's card, the touching sides are compared. If your number is higher, you capture their card (it turns your color). The player controlling the most cards when the board is full wins.

Reading a Card

Each card shows its state name, four directional values, and element badges. For example, a card might look like:

7 5 8 6 ← Top is 7, Right is 8, Bottom is 6, Left is 5

If this card is placed to the left of an opponent's card, your Right (8) is compared against their Left.

Elemental Advantages

Each element beats two others and loses to two. When comparing sides, if your element beats your opponent's, you get +1 to your value. If theirs beats yours, they get +1 to theirs.

Ocean beats Desert & Forest
Mountain beats Ocean & Plain
Desert beats Swamp & Forest
Plain beats Desert & Swamp
Forest beats Mountain & Plain
Swamp beats Ocean & Mountain

Advanced Rules

  • Same: If two or more of the placed card's sides match the touching sides of adjacent cards exactly, those opponent cards are captured.
  • Plus: If two or more pairs of touching sides have the same sum, those opponent cards are captured.
  • Combo: Cards captured by Same, Plus, or basic rules can chain-capture further adjacent opponent cards using basic comparison.

Scoring

Your score = cards you own on the board + cards left in your hand. The board has 9 cells and each player starts with 5 cards (10 total). The player with the higher score when the board is full wins.
